## 💬 Interview Experience

### Round 1 — Self Introduction

- Start with name, college, and branch.
- Mention key technical skills briefly.
- Highlight internships and projects.
- Keep it under 2 minutes.

---

### Round 2 — Project & Internship Discussion

- Interviewers spend **~30 minutes** on this alone.
- Be ready to explain architecture, tech stack, and your role.
- Expect questions on challenges faced and how you solved them.
- Internship at **TCS** and an **EdTech Startup** were discussed in depth.

> Know your project inside out. Every line, every decision.

---

### Round 3 — Paper Coding Round

- Code was written **on paper**, not on a computer.
- React implementation was asked as a scenario.
- Exact syntax was hard to recall under pressure.
- Candidate explained **logic and component flow** instead.
- Interviewer appreciated the approach over perfect syntax.

> Logic matters more than syntax. Communicate your thinking clearly.

---

### Round 4 — Core CS Questions

Topics asked:

- **Java** — concepts and syntax
- **OOPs** — inheritance, polymorphism, abstraction, encapsulation
- **DBMS** — normalization, joins, transactions
- **OS** — process management, scheduling, memory
- **CN** — protocols, OSI model, TCP/IP

---

### Round 5 — Managerial & HR Questions

- Scenario-based questions throughout.
- Questions on conflict resolution, deadlines, and teamwork.
- Answers backed by real internship and college experiences.

Common questions asked:

- *"How did you handle a conflict in your team?"*
- *"Describe a situation where you worked under pressure."*
- *"Why TCS?"*

> Use real experiences. Fabricated answers are easy to spot.

---

### Round 6 — DSA Question

- Asked at the end of the interview.
- Topic: **Palindrome String**
- Expected to explain approach before coding.

---

## 🔍 What Interviewers Focus On

| Area | What They Look For |
|---|---|
| Projects | Depth of understanding, not just surface knowledge |
| Coding | Logic and approach, even without perfect syntax |
| Core CS | Conceptual clarity across all subjects |
| Communication | Clarity, confidence, and honesty |
| HR | Real experiences, not textbook answers |

## 🏢 Employee Experience

### Work Culture

- Work varies widely by **project, client, and team**.
- Large teams with structured processes.
- Agile and automation practices followed in most projects.
- Collaborative environment in many divisions.

---

### 👍 What Employees Appreciate

- Strong **learning culture** through internal certifications and training.
- **Global exposure** via large enterprise clients.
- **Job stability** — very low risk of sudden layoffs.
- Good **work-life balance** in several teams.
- Ideal place for **freshers** to build a foundation.
- Opportunities to grow into **leadership and mentoring roles**.

---

### 👎 What Employees Struggle With

- **Salary hikes** are slower than industry standards.
- **Project allocation** is based on business needs, not preference.
- **Switching projects** internally is difficult.
- **Bureaucratic processes** slow down decisions.
- **Mentorship quality** varies heavily across teams.
- Limited **innovation** due to process-heavy workflows.
- **Internal politics** can affect growth in some teams.

---

## 🏁 Final Word

### Best Fit For

- Freshers entering the IT industry.
- Candidates who value stability over speed.
- Developers wanting enterprise-scale exposure.
- Professionals building a long-term career foundation.

### Not Ideal For

- Candidates expecting fast promotions.
- Professionals seeking aggressive salary growth.
- Engineers who prefer startup-like pace and flexibility.

---

> *Prepare well. Be honest. Know your projects deeply.*
